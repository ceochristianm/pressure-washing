# Sales Scripts

> Living document — after every 10 calls, note what's working/failing and revise.

## Inbound lead call (form leads — call within 1–5 min)
"Hi [Name], this is Christian with [Business] — you just requested a quote for your
home on [Street]. I've actually got your property pulled up on satellite right now.
[beat] I can see [specific: the driveway on the south side / the tile roof]. Let me
ask two quick things: is it mainly [siding/driveway/roof] you're wanting handled, or
the full exterior? ... Great — so we do this three ways: [3-tier pitch]. Most folks
in [neighborhood] go with the Curb Appeal package at $650, that covers X and Y.
I've got [day] morning or [day] afternoon open — which works better?"
Notes: always specific detail from satellite view (proof of effort), always assume
the close with a scheduling choice, never end without a booked slot or firm follow-up.

## Objection: "that's more than the other quote I got"
"Totally fair — you'll find guys who'll do it for $150. Here's the difference:
we're fully insured with a million-dollar policy — I'll text you the certificate
right now — and we soft-wash, which means chemicals do the work at low pressure.
High pressure is how vinyl siding gets shredded and window seals get blown, and
those repairs cost 10x the wash. You're not paying for cleaning, you're paying for
your home NOT getting damaged while it gets cleaned."

## The test-patch upsell (on-site)
"[Name], before I start on the siding — come look at this real quick. [run patch]
That's what's under the mold. When it rains, that black layer turns into an ice
rink — it's honestly a slip hazard. Normally the driveway's a $400 job on its own,
but my rig's already hooked up to your water, so I can knock the whole thing out
right now for an extra $200. Want me to add it?"

## 10-door knock (before starting a neighbor's job)
"Morning! I'm Christian — I'm about to wash [neighbor]'s house at [#] this morning,
so you'll see my truck out front. Since I'm already on the street with everything
set up, I'm doing a same-street rate for anyone who books this week — takes 2
minutes to quote from right here. Want me to take a look while I'm here?"

## Commercial walk-in (slip-and-fall pitch)
"Hi, is the manager around? ... I run a local exterior cleaning company — I noticed
your entrance concrete has heavy black mold buildup. When it rains that's a genuine
slip-and-fall liability at your front door. I can clean the entire entrance tonight
after you close for $350 so it's safe and bright for customers tomorrow morning."

## Review ask (on-site, wow-moment)
"Glad you love it! Quick favor — reviews are everything for a local business.
I'm texting you a link right now; it takes 30 seconds while you're looking at the
driveway. Mind knocking it out before I pull away?"
