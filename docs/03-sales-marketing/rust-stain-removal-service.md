# Specialty Service: Rust / Iron Stain Removal

> Added 2026-07-18. This is a DISTINCT service from standard soft-wash/pressure
> washing — different diagnosis, different chemical, different equipment, different
> pricing. Do not treat rust/iron staining with standard SH mix — it won't work.

## Diagnosis
Orange/rust-colored staining (as opposed to black/green algae streaking) is IRON
OXIDE from sprinkler/irrigation water hitting a surface repeatedly, oxidizing, and
chemically bonding into the pores of stucco, concrete, or siding. This is NOT
mold/algae and does not respond to sodium hypochlorite. High-pressure blasting can
etch/pit the surface and push iron deeper into the pores without removing the stain.

## Chemical
F9 BARC Rust and Oxidation Remover — industry-standard, ~$50/gallon concentrate.
Safe on concrete, pavers, stucco, siding, shingles. Dilute up to 1:3 (concentrate:
water) for non-concrete surfaces like stucco; less dilution for concrete. No
scrubbing needed — spray and let dwell.

## Equipment
A SEPARATE, dedicated pump sprayer (~$20-40 one-time purchase, hardware store) —
NEVER share with SH equipment. Mixing acid (F9 BARC/oxalic) with bleach residue
risks a hazardous chemical reaction (toxic gas). Keep this sprayer exclusively for
rust jobs going forward.

## Process
1. Walkthrough + photos
2. Plain water rinse ONLY IF surface has genuine dirt/grime beyond the rust
   staining itself — skip if just stained, not dirty
3. Flood landscaping with plain water
4. Mix F9 BARC in the dedicated sprayer per label dilution
5. Apply as even, light mist across the ENTIRE relevant surface, not just the
   stained patches — avoids blotchy boundary lines
6. Dwell per product instructions — no scrubbing
7. Final water-only rinse (rinsing only, not cleaning; no SH/surfactant used)
8. Final landscaping rinse
9. Walkthrough — set honest expectation staining may recur if sprinkler head
   isn't adjusted; suggest adjusting the angle
10. Review ask, before/after photos, payment, log
