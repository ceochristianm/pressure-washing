# Offline / Guerrilla Marketing

## Yard signs — BLITZES CUT (2026-07-17)
Intersection/median sign blitzes are cut from the plan: right-of-way placement is a
code violation in most PBC municipalities, HOAs strip them in hours, and the
30–50% confiscation rate makes ROI unreliable vs. putting the same money/attention
into Google PPC. The ONE sign play kept:
- **Customer's own yard, with permission, for ~1 week post-job** ($25-off trade).
  Legal, durable, and carries social proof. Track callers as source=`yard_sign`.

## Door hangers — job-perimeter ONLY (2026-07-17)
Mass drops cut (1,000 doors ≈ 8–12 hrs walking at 0.5–2% response — bad trade vs.
owner's hourly value). What's kept:
- **~10 nearest homes around every active job**, while the rig is running (15 min).
  These convert at multiples of cold drops — the rig on their street is the proof.
- Angle: "We're washing your neighbor's house at [#] today — same-street discount
  if booked this week." Specificity beats generic flyers. Target visibly dirty
  driveways/rooflines when choosing which doors.
- Order 250–500 printed (~$60–100), not 1,000+.

## The 10-door knock (gated communities)
- Timing: BEFORE starting the job (fresh, not sweaty; morning knocks OK — retirees
  and WFH answer). The running rig later is the credibility proof for anyone who watched.
- Script in sales-scripts.md.

## Commercial walk-ins (cash-flow filler, 3/day baseline)
- Strip malls/restaurants/gas stations with filthy entrances
- Pitch = slip-and-fall liability, not aesthetics (script in sales-scripts.md)
- NOTE: commercial requires the CCC insurance endorsement + is sales-taxable —
  fine for small storefront jobs, defer big contracts to Month 3+.
