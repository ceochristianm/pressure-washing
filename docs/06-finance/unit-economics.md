# Unit Economics

## Per-job model (residential, verify against actuals in data/jobs.csv)
- Variable costs (SH, surfactant, fuel, wear): ~8–12% of ticket → est. $45–75 on a $550 job
- Solo labor: 2–4 hrs on-site early (target < 2.5 hrs with reps) + drive time
- Contribution margin per $550 job: ~$480–500 before ads/overhead

## Channel math (the numbers that decide everything)
- Cost per booked job (CPJ) = channel spend / jobs booked from channel
- Break-even CPJ on a $550 job ≈ $480 (absurd headroom) — but the REAL bar is
  opportunity + payback speed: target CPJ < $100 paid channels
- Free channels (Marketplace/signs/knocks): CPJ ≈ time only → why they lead the launch

## Break-even snapshot (launch)
- Total launch outlay ~$2,100–3,000 → covered by 4–6 jobs at $550 AJS
- Meaning: the equipment pays for itself inside the first two weeks of even slow
  job flow. The risk isn't the economics — it's execution speed vs. the Amex clock.

## Levers ranked by profit impact
1. AJS (upsell attach rate) — $350 vs $650 job = same drive, same setup, ~2x profit
2. Speed-to-lead (close rate multiplier on every channel simultaneously)
3. Route density (jobs per day; ZIP concentration strategy)
4. Chemical discipline (overspray waste is the classic rookie leak)
