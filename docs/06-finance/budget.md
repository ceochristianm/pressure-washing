# Budget Rules — The Amex Constraint

## The instrument
Amex Gold CHARGE card, ~$5,500 available. **Full balance due in 30–60 days — no
revolving.** This is the governing constraint on every spend decision.

## Rules
1. **Ceiling: $3,000 total spend until revenue arrives.** The remaining ~$2,500 is
   untouchable buffer.
2. **Every charge starts a payback clock.** Before any spend: "does projected cash
   inside the payback window cover this + everything already on the card?"
3. Ads scale only on collected-revenue evidence (cost per booked job < $100), never
   on lead volume alone.
4. Revenue priority order: (1) Amex balance, (2) 1-month operating buffer,
   (3) growth spend (ads/equipment), (4) owner pay.

## Launch allocation (target)
| Bucket | Amount |
|---|---|
| Equipment (used-first) | $1,400–2,000 |
| Insurance month 1 | $50–95 |
| Yard signs + flyers + misc | ~$150 |
| Phone/automation stack | ~$35 |
| Meta ads (post-gate) | $500–750 to start |
| **Total** | **$2,135–3,030** — at ceiling; used-equipment savings widen the ad room |

## Weekly cash check (part of weekly review)
- Amex balance vs. days remaining vs. cash on hand
- If projected collections < balance due at T-10 days: freeze ads immediately,
  shift to free channels + collections push. This rule is automatic, not a judgment call.
