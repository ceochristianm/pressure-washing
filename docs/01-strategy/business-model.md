# Business Model

## Purpose
Pressure washing = fast-cash bridge + systemized asset. Conversion Consulting (the
agency) = the long-term $100K+/mo play. This business exists to (1) generate income
now using existing marketing/sales skills, (2) become largely self-running by
~September 2026 so focus returns to the agency, (3) optionally become a sellable
asset (customer database + reviews + systems have standalone value).

## The edge
Most local operators are great at washing, terrible at marketing. Christian is the
inverse — and marketing/sales/systems are the hard part. Ads, copy, offers, speed-to-lead,
automation: already known. The trade itself is learnable in weeks.

## Model mechanics
- High-margin local service: variable costs (chemicals, fuel, soap) ≈ 8–10% of ticket
- Premium positioning: $550+ average job via 3-tier menu + on-site upsell — NOT
  competing on price with $99-driveway operators
- Recurring flywheel: exteriors re-grow algae annually in FL → database reactivation
  = near-free repeat revenue
- Built to exit/delegate from day 1: video SOPs, CRM data ownership, strict account
  separation

## Phase gates (not calendar-worship — each phase requires the prior one's proof)
| Phase | Entry condition | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Launch (now) | Equipment + insurance | First 5 jobs free channels, learn trade, capture assets |
| 2. Paid growth | 2–3 jobs done + speed-to-lead live | Meta ads on, $550+ AJS discipline, 12–15 jobs/mo |
| 3. Leverage | Consistent job flow + cash cushion | Hire driver/tech, onboard VA, step off truck |
| 4. Remote (target ~Sept) | Crew stable 2+ weeks without intervention | Medellín, ~2 hrs/day management, agency focus |
