# Legal Setup: Insurance First, LLC Second

> Sequence decided 2026-07-07: launch as a sole proprietor with a monthly general
> liability policy, then file the LLC within 30–60 days. Reasoning below.

## Why Insurance Before LLC

- **Insurance pays for damage** (broken window, dead landscaping, etched concrete) —
  the realistic day-1 risk. An LLC pays for nothing; it only shields personal assets
  in a catastrophic lawsuit.
- An LLC without insurance = business is bankrupt after its first serious accident anyway.
- Sole proprietorship exists automatically the moment payment is accepted — zero
  filing needed to start legally.
- Cost/speed: insurance = ~$30–70 first month, active in ~10 minutes online.
  Florida LLC = $125 + processing days.

## Step 1 — General Liability Insurance (BEFORE first job, this week)

- **Providers to quote (all instant/online):** NEXT Insurance, Thimble, Progressive
  Commercial. Answer ~5–10 questions (ZIP, est. revenue, equipment), instant COI PDF.
- **Coverage: $1M policy.** Expect **$30–75/month** for a solo, no-claims operator.
- **Two options at the very start:**
  - *Monthly policy* (recommended): continuous cover, can add endorsements, supports
    "licensed & insured" marketing year-round.
  - *Thimble day-policies* (~$6–10/day): viable for the literal first weekend if cash
    is ultra-tight, but upgrade to monthly by job 3–5.
- **The CCC trap (important):** basic GL policies exclude "Care, Custody, and Control" —
  damage to the exact surface being worked on (e.g., wand marks carved into a deck,
  etched concrete). Ask each insurer about a **CCC / Customer Property Protection
  endorsement** (~$15–20/mo extra). Given zero trade experience at launch, this
  endorsement is worth having from day 1.
- **Height check:** confirm the policy doesn't exclude roof work — roof soft-washing
  is a core Tier 3 service.
- Keep the digital COI on the phone; wealthy homeowners and every gated community
  gate will ask for it.

## Step 2 — Florida LLC (within 30–60 days of first job, or sooner on triggers)

**File immediately if ANY of these happen first:**
- Hiring anyone (even casual weekend help beyond dad)
- Bidding commercial work (they require entity + continuous policy + additional-insured COIs)
- Steady profit worth legally isolating

**The filing process (DIY, ~15 min, don't pay LegalZoom):**
1. **Name check** on sunbiz.org (also check domain + IG handle at the same time)
2. **File Articles of Organization** at sunbiz.org — **$125**
3. **EIN** at irs.gov — free, 5 minutes, immediately after LLC approval
4. **Business checking account** (bring LLC approval + EIN). From that moment:
   every business dollar in/out of this account ONLY. Commingling funds pierces
   the LLC's liability protection.
5. Update the insurance policy to list the LLC as the named insured.

## Step 3 — Upgrades That Come Later (revenue-gated)

| Trigger | Add |
|---|---|
| Hired driver touches the truck | Commercial auto policy + list driver as authorized |
| First employee (W-2) | Workers' comp (FL requirement varies by employee count/type — verify current rule when hiring) |
| Equipment worth protecting from theft | Tools & equipment coverage rider |
| Commercial bids | $1M/$2M limits + CCC endorsement + additional-insured capability (free on NEXT/Thimble dashboards) |

## Florida Sales Tax Cheat Sheet

| Work type | Tax status |
|---|---|
| Residential exterior washing (homes, HOAs, duplexes) | **EXEMPT** — charge $0 tax (FL Admin Code 12A-1.0091) |
| Commercial exterior washing | **Taxable** — 6% + Palm Beach County surtax |
| Holiday light install/lease (if pursued) | **Taxable** — tangible property lease |

Month 1–2 residential-only = zero sales tax bookkeeping. Revisit when commercial starts.
