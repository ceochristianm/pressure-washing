# Equipment Buying Guide

> Goal: a professional-capable residential soft-wash rig for **$1,400–$2,000 all-in**,
> leaving $1,000+ of the $3K ceiling for insurance + first marketing. Used-first strategy.

## The Shopping List (in priority order)

### 1. Pressure washer — THE core purchase
**Target: used commercial gas unit, 4.0 GPM / ~4,000 PSI, Honda GX390 engine**

- **Used price target: $500–$900** on FB Marketplace / Craigslist / OfferUp
  (new equivalent: $1,100–$1,400 at Northern Tool or a local pressure supply house)
- Why this spec: 4 GPM is the minimum "pro" flow rate — GPM (rinse speed) matters more
  than PSI for house washing. Honda GX390 is the industry-standard engine: bulletproof,
  parts everywhere, holds resale value.
- Why used is safe HERE specifically: GX390s routinely run thousands of hours. The pump
  is the wear item, and a replacement pump is ~$300–400 — so even a bad-pump deal can be
  fine if priced accordingly.
- **Avoid:** anything electric, anything under 3.5 GPM, big-box residential brands
  (Ryobi, Sun Joe, Greenworks), "Predator" engines only if heavily discounted.

**Used-unit inspection checklist (bring cash, test before paying):**
1. Cold start test — insist the seller does NOT pre-warm it. Hard cold starts = carb issues.
2. Check oil (engine AND pump) — milky oil = water intrusion = walk away.
3. Run it under pressure for 5 full minutes — watch for pressure pulsing/surging
   (unloader or pump valve problems).
4. Check for pump leaks at the manifold while running.
5. Look at the frame/skid for rust cancer (light surface rust fine; flaking structural rust no).
6. Ask WHY they're selling — "upgraded rigs" = good sign; vague answers + very low
   hours claim = suspicious.
7. Haggle: listed $900 → offer $700 cash-in-hand. Used equipment market always negotiates.

### 2. 12V soft-wash setup — for roofs and delicate siding
**Target: $250–$400 built from parts (buy new — cheap enough that used isn't worth risk)**

- 12V diaphragm pump, 5.5–7.0 GPM (Remco, Delavan, or NorthStar): $150–250 new
- Deep-cycle marine battery: $100–130 (Walmart EverStart is fine)
- 100 ft of 1/2" clear braided chemical-rated hose: ~$60
- Basic poly spray gun/wand for chemical application: ~$30

### 3. Surface cleaner — the driveway money-maker
**Target: used Whisper Wash Classic or similar 19–20", $250–$400 used ($500–700 new)**

- This is the tool that makes driveways fast AND is the physical prop for the
  test-patch upsell. Non-negotiable purchase.
- Used check: spin the bar by hand (should spin freely), inspect swivel for leaks,
  check nozzles aren't hollowed out from wear.

### 4. Hoses, reels, fittings
**Target: $200–$350**

- 100–150 ft of 3/8" non-marking high-pressure hose (new, ~$120–180 — don't buy used
  pressure hose, hidden wear = burst risk = injury)
- Downstream chemical injector + J-rod nozzle set: ~$50–70
- Quick-connect fittings kit: ~$30
- Skip powered hose reels for now ($1,500+) — hand-coil month 1, buy reels with revenue.

### 5. Chemicals + safety + misc
**Target: $200–$300**

- 12.5% sodium hypochlorite (SH): buy at a local pool supply store — 2–4 five-gal
  carboys to start (~$80–120). Do NOT store in enclosed spaces or the truck cab.
- Surfactant ("snot" soap, e.g. Southern Drawl / Slo Mo): 1 gal, ~$40
- Safety: chemical-rated goggles, respirator with organic vapor cartridges, nitrile
  gloves, rubber boots (~$80). Not optional — SH mist damages lungs and eyes.
- Painter's tape + plastic sheeting (outlet masking), 2 garden hoses (~$50)

### 6. Skid platform for the borrowed truck
**Target: $60–$100 DIY**

- Marine-grade plywood sheet sized between the Silverado's wheel wells
- Bolt equipment to the plywood, NOT the truck
- 4+ heavy ratchet straps to factory tie-down loops
- **Weight discipline:** no big water tank in a half-ton bed. Run off the homeowner's
  spigot; carry only 15–25 gal chemical tanks. (Payload ~1,600–2,000 lbs total.)

## Budget Summary

| Item | Used-first target | New fallback |
|---|---|---|
| 4 GPM gas washer (GX390) | $500–900 | $1,100–1,400 |
| 12V soft-wash kit | $250–400 (new parts) | same |
| Surface cleaner | $250–400 used | $500–700 |
| Hoses/fittings | $200–350 (hose new) | same |
| Chemicals + safety | $200–300 | same |
| Skid + straps | $60–100 | same |
| **TOTAL** | **$1,460–$2,450** | **$2,310–$2,950** |

Decision rule: hunt used for the washer + surface cleaner for 3–5 days max. If nothing
good surfaces in Palm Beach County listings by then, buy new at the fallback prices
rather than stalling the launch — time-to-first-job matters more than saving $400.

## Where to Buy

- **Used:** Facebook Marketplace (search "pressure washer commercial", "4 gpm",
  "Honda GX390", "surface cleaner" within 50 mi), Craigslist, OfferUp. Contractors
  upgrading rigs are the ideal sellers.
- **New/parts:** Northern Tool (West Palm Beach area), local pressure washing supply
  distributors (search "pressure washer supply near me" — S. FL has several pro shops;
  they also sell SH cheaper in bulk than pool stores), Amazon for fittings/injectors only.
- **Chemicals:** local pool supply (SH), pro supply shop (surfactant).

## What NOT to Buy Yet (revenue-gated purchases)

- Powered hose reels ($1,500+) — Month 2+
- Buffer tank + belt-drive upgrade — only when moving to trailer
- Trailer ($1,500–3,500 used) — when Silverado situation changes or crew scales
- Water reclamation system — commercial work only
- Hot water burner — commercial/grease work only
