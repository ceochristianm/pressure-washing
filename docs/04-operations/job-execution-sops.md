# Job Execution SOPs

> Safety and property-protection first — one landscaping kill or blown window
> erases multiple jobs of profit and the insurance deductible.

## Chemical mix matrix
| Surface | Mix | Application |
|---|---|---|
| Vinyl siding / stucco / painted wood | 1–1.5% SH + surfactant | Downstream injector, LOW pressure |
| Tile / shingle roofs | 3–4% SH + heavy surfactant | 12V pump only — NEVER pressure on a roof |
| Concrete driveways/walks | Pre-treat ~2% SH → surface cleaner → post-treat | 4 GPM machine + surface cleaner |

SH handling: 12.5% pool-grade. Never mix with other chemicals (ammonia/acids = toxic
gas). Ventilated storage only, never in a cab. Goggles + respirator when mixing/spraying.

## Pre-job protocol (20 min, EVERY job)
1. Walkthrough WITH homeowner: photograph pre-existing damage (cracked windows, dead
   plants, loose trim) — timestamped phone photos = damage-claim protection
2. Tape + plastic over all exterior outlets, doorbells, cameras, fixtures
3. FLOOD all landscaping/grass/roots with fresh water before any chemical —
   saturated plants can't absorb bleach
4. Confirm spigot works + note water pressure
5. Close windows reminder to homeowner; pets inside

## During-job rules
- Dedicated rinse hose running over plants whenever chemical is being applied nearby
- Never point high pressure at: windows, door seals, light fixtures, screens, soffit
  vents, vehicles
- Roofs: chemical + gravity only. Walking tile roofs breaks tiles — treat from
  ladder/edge wherever possible
- Test-patch upsell moment: right after hookup (script in sales-scripts.md)

## Post-job protocol
1. Final landscape rinse (thorough)
2. Remove all tape/plastic
3. Walkthrough with homeowner → wow moment → review ask + before/after photos
4. Collect payment on-site (tap-to-pay)
5. Same-day: log job in data/jobs.csv (price, costs, time, upsell, ZIP, source)

## Rookie self-protection rules (month 1)
- Book max 2 jobs/day until a full job takes < 2.5 hrs consistently
- When unsure about a surface: test in an inconspicuous spot first
- Wind check before roof chemical — overspray drift onto cars/neighbors is the
  classic catastrophic rookie claim
